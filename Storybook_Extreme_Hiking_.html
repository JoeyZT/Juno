Early the next day, Mr Munchit and Mr Bright set off early for a hike. They both had big rucksacks with them. They wanted to climb Pat Sin Leng.
第二天一早，蒙奇特先生和布萊特先生就出發去遠足。他們都背著大大的背包，想要攀登八仙嶺。
‘OK, up we go!’ said Mr Bright. ‘Extreme Hiking!’
「好，我們出發吧！」布萊特先生說。「極限遠足！」
‘Oh dear, it looks very high!’ sighed Mr Munchit. But they set off cheerfully.
「天啊，看起來好高啊！」蒙奇特先生嘆了口氣。但他們還是開心地出發了。
Puff! Puff! Pant! Pant! Up they went. After about an hour the two friends reached the first peak.
呼！呼！喘！喘！他們一路往上爬。大約一小時後，兩位朋友抵達了第一個山頂。
Oh, my! Is it much further?
哦，天啊！還要走很遠嗎？
Then the next… And the next…
然後下一座……再下一座……
When they got home, it was already late.
當他們回到家時，天已經很晚了。
During the week Mr Bright did lots of ironing.
那一整週，布萊特先生都在瘋狂熨衣服。
Every Sunday Mr Munchit and Mr Bright climbed the steepest, highest hills around.
每個星期天，蒙奇特先生和布萊特先生都會去爬附近最陡、最高的山。
And Mr Munchit cooked busily every day.
而蒙奇特先生每天都在忙著做飯。
One Saturday Mrs Munchit found her husband writing e-mails and his friend Mr Bright making phone calls.
某個星期六，蒙奇特太太發現她的丈夫在寫電子郵件，而他的朋友布萊特先生在打電話。
‘What are you two doing?’ she asked.
「你們兩個在做什麼？」她問道。
‘Oh, nothing dear,’ said Mr Munchit. He winked at Mr Bright.
「哦，沒什麼，親愛的。」蒙奇特先生說著，對布萊特先生眨了眨眼。
The next morning Mr Munchit got up early to meet his friend as usual.
第二天早上，蒙奇特先生像往常一樣早起去見他的朋友。
‘That’s a very large rucksack, dear,’ said Mrs Munchit as her husband left.
「親愛的，你的背包好大啊。」蒙奇特先生離開時，蒙奇特太太說道。
‘He really is behaving quite strangely these days,’ she thought to herself.
「他最近的行為真的很奇怪。」她在心裡想。
It was hard work climbing the hill. Mr Bright also had a very large rucksack.
爬山很辛苦。布萊特先生也背著一個非常大的背包。
When they got to the top, there were a lot of people there already. There was even a helicopter circling in the sky!
當他們到達山頂時，那裡已經有很多人了。天空中甚至還有一架直升機在盤旋！
At home Mrs Munchit was having a cup of tea. It was almost lunchtime, time for the news.
在家裡，蒙奇特太太正在喝茶。差不多到午餐時間，也是看新聞的時間。
She sat down in her favourite armchair, pressed the remote and dipped a biscuit in her tea.
她坐在最喜歡的扶手椅上，按著遙控器，把餅乾浸在茶裡。
‘Oh, my goodness,’ said Mrs Munchit. On the screen she saw her husband and Mr Bright walking up a hill. There were lots of people cheering.
「哦，我的天啊！」蒙奇特太太說。在螢幕上，她看見丈夫和布萊特先生正在爬山，有很多人在歡呼。
Mr Munchit put down his rucksack and start to pull things out. There was a camping stove… a wok… some oil… a box of eggs…
蒙奇特先生放下背包，開始拿出東西。有露營爐、鑊、油、一盒雞蛋……
Then Mr Bright put down his rucksack and pulled out an iron… and a small ironing board. Then he turned his bag upside down and shook out a huge pile of socks.
然後布萊特先生放下背包，拿出熨斗和小型燙衣板。接著他把袋子倒過來，倒出一大堆襪子。
‘Oooh!’ gasped Mrs Munchit. People crowded round Mr Munchit and Mr Bright. There were photographers, cameramen, reporters from the TV. Everyone was very excited.
「哇！」蒙奇特太太倒吸一口氣。人們圍住蒙奇特先生和布萊特先生，有攝影師、攝製人員、電視台記者，每個人都很興奮。
‘And now,’ said Mr Munchit. ‘For the first time ever… Extreme Omelette‑making in Hong Kong!’ He knelt down, lit the stove and started to cook.
「現在，」蒙奇特先生說，「香港有史以來第一次……山頂極限煎奄列！」他跪下，點燃爐子，開始烹飪。
The cameras flashed and people gasped.
相機閃光不斷，人們驚呼。
‘And another first for Hong Kong…’ said Mr Bright, ‘… Extreme Sock‑ironing!’
「香港另一項創舉……」布萊特先生說，「……山頂極限熨襪！」
The people on TV cheered. Mrs Munchit put down her tea.
電視上的人們歡呼著。蒙奇特太太放下茶杯。
‘Well, well,’ she said to herself. ‘Hmm…’
「哎呀呀，」她自言自語，「嗯……」
